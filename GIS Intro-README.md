GIS Introduction
-------------------
I am an emerging GIS practitioner currently pursuing a Geographic Information Systems (GIS) certificate, with a strong foundation in spatial data collection, analysis, and interpretation. In addition to my academic training, I hold an FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificate, which has provided me with practical experience in acquiring high-quality geospatial data using unmanned aerial systems (UAS). This combination of technical coursework and hands-on fieldwork has allowed me to develop a well-rounded skill set in both data acquisition and spatial analysis workflows.

I am particularly drawn to hydrological analysis and the broader application of geospatial technologies to complex environmental and planning challenges. My interests include identifying water access points, supporting land surveying efforts, contributing to mineral and resource detection, and applying GIS in emergency response planning and hazard assessment. I am also interested in environmentally responsible development and renewable energy planning. Across these areas, I aim to leverage spatial data to aid and support informed, data-driven decision-making processes.

Ultimately, I am motivated by the opportunity to use GIS as a tool for meaningful impact. Whether working at the local level to support community planning initiatives or contributing to international environmental and infrastructure projects, I aim to apply spatial analysis to address real-world challenges. My goal is to continue developing my technical capabilities while contributing to projects that promote sustainable development and improved resource access.
